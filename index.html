Predictive Analysis for Urban Transportation: NYC Taxi Data

Project Description: The final project applies machine learning techniques to NYC Yellow Taxi trip data to predict fare and trip duration, understand tipping behavior, and forecast demand. The project integrates regression, classification, clustering, and time-series models to identify spatial and temporal patterns in taxi activity. After cleaning and preprocessing a sample of 500,000 records, the team developed predictive models to support smarter fleet deployment, optimized taxi dispatching, and data-driven urban mobility strategies.

My Role: Collected and cleaned the raw NYC Yellow Taxi dataset, reducing it to a high-quality sample of 487,321 trips by removing outliers (e.g., invalid coordinates, zero fares, extreme trip durations/speeds). Led data preprocessing efforts including time-based, location-based, and contextual feature engineering (e.g., rush hour indicators, airport flags). Applied transformations to normalize skewed variables and standardize features. Also conducted in-depth exploratory data analysis and built visualizations to uncover key patterns in fare, duration, and tipping behavior.

Key Technologies & Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn (Linear Regression, XGBoost, K-Means), Statsmodels/SciPy, Prophet, SARIMA, Jupyter Notebook, NYC Taxi Trip Data.

Impact & Learning: Gained hands-on experience working with large-scale real-world transportation data and applying rigorous data preprocessing techniques.
